Mushroom Soup

Description

Enjoy a nice pot of hot mushroom soup on a cold winter day! This recipe is simple with few ingredients based off a recipe by Anthony Bourdain. It is quick to throw together, but does take time for the flavors to develop. Not much work, and very worth the wait!

Ingredients

  • 6 Tablespoons of butter
  • 1 Onion sliced
  • 12 oz sliced button mushrooms
  • 4-6 oz whole button mushrooms
  • 4 cups chicken stock
  • 1-2 sprigs of parsely
  • 2 ounces of sherry
  • 4 Tablespoons olive oil
  • salt & pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Melt 2 tablespoons of butter into a pan at medium heat and lightly saute onion until just beginning to become transluscent. (approximately 3-5 mins)
  2. Add remaining butter and sliced mushrooms. Continue to cook for 8 minutes.
  3. Stir in stock and parsley and bring mixture to a boil.
  4. Reduce to a simmer and cook for 1 hour.
  5. While soup is simmering, preheat oven to 425 F.
  6. Toss whole mushrooms in olive oil, salt and pepper.
  7. Spread whole mushrooms on baking sheet and bake 4-5 minutes until just beginning to brown.
  8. After soup has simmered for 1 hour, blend in blender or use immersion blender to make soup creamy.
  9. Add whole mushrooms from oven to soup as a garnish, and enjoy!

Note: when I used an immersion blender, the soup was not as creamy as I wouldve liked. It seemed slightly gritty. In the future, I plan to alter this recipe to instead of sliced, dice mushrooms real small and not blend them. I will add 1/2 cup of cream or milk, and 2 tablespoons of flour in the last 10 minutes of the saute to make it creamier.
